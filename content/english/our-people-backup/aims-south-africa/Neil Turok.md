Professor Neil Turok, a South African-born theoretical physicist, founded AIMS in 2003. He is best known for his work in cosmology, including the Ekpyrotic and Cyclic Universe models. He has served as Chair of Mathematical Physics at the University of Cambridge and Director of the Perimeter Institute for Theoretical Physics in Canada. In 2008, he was awarded the TED Prize, which he used to support the expansion of AIMS across Africa.
